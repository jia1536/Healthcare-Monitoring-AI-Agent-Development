# Common Cold vs Influenza (Flu)

Both are respiratory illnesses caused by viruses but differ in severity and onset.

## Common Cold
- Gradual onset
- Symptoms: runny/stuffy nose, sneezing, mild sore throat, mild cough
- Rarely causes fever in adults
- Usually resolves in 7-10 days
- Managed with rest, fluids, and over-the-counter symptom relief

## Influenza (Flu)
- Sudden onset
- Symptoms: high fever, body aches, fatigue, dry cough, headache, chills
- Can lead to complications like pneumonia, especially in high-risk groups
- Antiviral medications may help if started early, prescribed by a doctor

## When to Seek Medical Care
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent chest pain or pressure
- Confusion
- Severe or persistent vomiting
- Symptoms that improve then return with fever and worse cough
- High-risk individuals (elderly, pregnant, chronic conditions, infants) with flu symptoms
