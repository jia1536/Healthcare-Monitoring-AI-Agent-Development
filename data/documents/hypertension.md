# Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Hypertension occurs when the force of blood against artery walls stays consistently too high.

## Blood Pressure Categories (approximate, general guidance)
- Normal: below 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129 / below 80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139 / 80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: 140+ / 90+ mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: above 180/120 mmHg — seek emergency care

## Common Symptoms
Hypertension is often called a "silent" condition because it may have no symptoms. When present, symptoms can include:
- Headaches
- Shortness of breath
- Nosebleeds
- Dizziness

## Risk Factors
- Family history
- High salt intake
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Obesity
- Smoking and excess alcohol
- Stress

## Management
- Reduce sodium intake
- Regular physical activity
- Maintain a healthy weight
- Limit alcohol and quit smoking
- Medication as prescribed by a doctor
- Routine blood pressure monitoring
