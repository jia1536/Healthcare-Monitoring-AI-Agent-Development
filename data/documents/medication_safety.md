# General Medication Safety

## Best Practices
- Always follow the prescribed dosage and schedule
- Never share prescription medications with others
- Store medications away from direct sunlight, heat, and moisture
- Keep medications out of reach of children
- Check expiry dates regularly and dispose of expired medication safely
- Do not stop a prescribed medication course early without consulting a doctor

## Common Interaction Considerations
- Combining certain painkillers (e.g., multiple NSAIDs) can increase side-effect risk
- Alcohol can interact with many medications, including sedatives and some antibiotics
- Some blood pressure and diabetes medications interact with over-the-counter cold remedies
- Always inform your doctor or pharmacist about ALL medications and supplements you take, including herbal or Ayurvedic remedies

## Adherence Tips
- Use pill organizers or reminder apps
- Link medication times to daily habits (e.g., meals, brushing teeth)
- Keep an updated medication list, including dosages, for emergencies

## Disclaimer
This information is for general educational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Never adjust or combine medications without consulting a licensed doctor or pharmacist.
